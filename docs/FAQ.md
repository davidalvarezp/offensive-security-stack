# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Is this a real hacking tool?
No.
OSS is a **simulation-based educational framework** designed for learning.

---

## Can this be used on the internet?
No.
OSS is intended only for **isolated lab environments**.

---

## Does OSS contain exploits?
No.
It includes **demonstrations, simulations, and analysis tools only**.

---

## Is this legal to use?
Yes — **when used properly**:
- With authorization
- In controlled environments
- For education or research

Misuse may be illegal.

---

## Why is this written in Go?
Go provides:
- Strong concurrency
- Static binaries
- Cross-platform support
- Excellent tooling for security education

---

## Can I add real exploit modules?
No.
Such contributions will be rejected.

---

## Can I use this to teach students?
Yes — this is the primary goal.

---

## Is this similar to Kali Linux?
Conceptually, yes — but:
- OSS is **educational-first**
- Safer
- Modular
- Instructor-controlled

---

## How do I contribute?
- Follow the Code of Conduct
- Respect ethical boundaries
- Submit educational improvements
- Avoid offensive automation

---

## Where can I ask questions?
- GitHub Discussions
- Documentation
- Your instructor or institution
